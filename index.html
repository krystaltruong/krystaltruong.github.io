Clubhouse
Team: 5 Designers
1st out of 70+ participants ☺
View Prototype ↗
Challenge
Create a privacy-conscious product, system, or service to reduce the negative impact of the physical and mental barriers that people can face during social distancing.
Solution
Design a web platform that allows elementary school students to form connections with other students in a privacy-concious after-school environment.
Understanding the project and developing a strategy
On Clubhouse, students will be able to meet individuals that they never would’ve had the opportunity to meet before and bond over shared interests by creating mini-communities, or “clubs”. Clubhouse revives students’ sense of belonging and connection, contributing to better physical and mental health.

Keeping privacy and security in mind, no student information is collected other than what is already collected by their school board, such as student IDs and emails. Students’ usernames and profile pictures will be anonymized, and students under 16 will require parental consent.
Pebble
Team: 3 Designers
3rd out of 900+ participants
View Prototype ↗
Challenge
How can we help adults who are new to gaming discover video games that fit their interests and schedules through a proficiency-focused approach?
Solution
Design a mobile app to create a gamified, educational experience that encourages adults to explore game titles, storyline and development while promoting discovery and community amongst gamers.
Understanding the project and developing a strategy
The huge world of gaming can be intimidating, so Pebble’s gamified process puts community first and strives to understand each user’s unique situation through a personalized onboarding process. This way, users can receive tailored game recommendations and be connected with a community of gamers with similar goals and interests. Additionally, hectic schedules can make it difficult to get into gaming, so Pebble offers the feature to send gaming invites to friends with similar availability.
